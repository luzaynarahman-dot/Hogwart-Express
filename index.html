Welcome to Hogwarts
School of Witchcraft & Wizardry

Nestled in the Scottish Highlands, Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry stands as the most prestigious magical institution in the wizarding world. Founded over a thousand years ago by Godric Gryffindor, Helga Hufflepuff, Rowena Ravenclaw, and Salazar Slytherin, our castle has been home to countless witches and wizards who have shaped the course of magical history.

From the enchanted ceiling of the Great Hall to the mysterious depths of the Forbidden Forest, every corner of Hogwarts holds secrets waiting to be discovered. Here, young witches and wizards learn to master their magical abilities, forge lifelong friendships, and embark on extraordinary adventures that will define their legacy.

Explore Hogwarts

Magical Gallery

Every corner of Hogwarts holds a secret waiting to be discovered. Step into the magic...

Great Hall

The Heart of Hogwarts

The magnificent Great Hall is where students gather for feasts, Sorting Ceremonies, and daily meals. Four long house tables stretch beneath the enchanted ceiling, which perfectly mirrors the sky outside—rain or shine, day or night.

Magical Fact

The ceiling was enchanted by Professor Flitwick to replicate the night sky. Over 1,000 floating candles illuminate the hall, maintained by a permanent charm.

Entrance Hall, 1st Floor

Hogwarts Library

Where Knowledge Lives

Thousands of magical books line the towering shelves, some of which bite or scream when opened. The Restricted Section holds the most dangerous tomes, accessible only with a signed permission slip.

Magical Fact

Madame Irma Pince, the librarian, has enchanted the books to return themselves to their proper shelves. The Restricted Section contains books like "Secrets of the Darkest Art" and "Magick Moste Evile."

Second Floor, West Wing

Astronomy Tower

Reach for the Stars

The tallest tower at Hogwarts, where students study the stars, planets, and celestial movements. Astronomy classes are held here at midnight, using powerful telescopes to map the night sky.

Magical Fact

Professor Aurora Sinistra teaches Astronomy here. The tower offers the best view of the Milky Way and has witnessed many important moments in Hogwarts history, including Dumbledore's tragic fall.

Highest Tower, West Side

Gryffindor Common Room

Where Bravery Finds Home

A warm, cozy circular room filled with squashy armchairs and a crackling fireplace. Located in Gryffindor Tower behind the portrait of the Fat Lady, accessible only with the correct password.

Magical Fact

The Fat Lady guards the entrance and has been known to leave her portrait for parties. The common room offers stunning views of the Quidditch pitch and Black Lake.

Gryffindor Tower, 7th Floor

Forbidden Forest

Mystery & Danger

A dark, ancient forest on Hogwarts grounds, strictly off-limits to students. Home to centaurs, unicorns, Acromantulas, and countless other magical creatures. Only Hagrid dares to enter regularly.

Magical Fact

The forest is home to the centaur colony led by Firenze and the Acromantula colony led by Aragog's descendants. Unicorns are often spotted near the edge of the forest.

Hogwarts Grounds, Northern Edge

Chamber of Secrets

Legend of Salazar Slytherin

A hidden chamber built by Salazar Slytherin, accessible only to Parselmouths. It once held the Basilisk and has been a legendary mystery for centuries.

Magical Fact

The entrance is marked by a small snake on a faucet in Moaning Myrtle's bathroom. The chamber contains a massive statue of Salazar Slytherin and the shed skin of the Basilisk.

Deep Beneath Hogwarts

Herbology Greenhouse

Where Magic Grows

A series of glass structures where students learn to care for magical plants. From Mandrakes to Venomous Tentacula, these greenhouses house the most fascinating and dangerous flora in the wizarding world.

Magical Fact

Professor Pomona Sprout teaches Herbology here. Greenhouses 1-3 are for younger students, while Greenhouse 4 houses the most dangerous plants. Mandrakes grown here are used in restorative potions.

Behind Castle, South Grounds

The Owlery

Wings of Communication

A tall stone tower where hundreds of owls roost, waiting to deliver mail to students and staff. The Owlery is accessible via a spiral staircase and offers a stunning view of the Hogwarts grounds.

Magical Fact

The school maintains a flock of owls for student use, but many bring their own. Hedwig, Harry Potter's snowy owl, was a famous resident here. The owls instinctively know where to deliver mail.

West Tower, Near Viaduct
